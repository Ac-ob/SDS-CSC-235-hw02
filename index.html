Dataset information

This dataset contains calls made to the Urban Park Rangers for wildlife emergencies in New York City. The data was collected by the Department of Parks and Recreation (DPR). Each row is a response from the Urban Park Rangers. Bias might arise due to the lack of specificity of the time unit used for the Duration_of_response variable. It is unclear what the time unit is, and different people can interpret it as different time units.

Hypothesis (hover over hypothesis1, hypothesis2, and hypothesis3 if you need a reminder of what graph inspired the hypothesis)


Hypothesis1:

  • independence: The duration of a call is independent from the number of animals injured
  • non-independence: The duration of a call is dependent on the number of animals injured

We believe duration of a call and number of animals injured on the event are independent from each other based on our visual analysis of the scatterplot for these two variables.

Hypothesis2:

  • Hnull: The number of animals rehabilitated per year in NY has remained constant throught the years
  • Ha: The number of animals rehabilitated per year has significantly increased throught the years

We believe rehabilitations per year have significantly increased based on the steep increase of rehabilitations after 2018

Hypothesis3:

  • Hnull: The number of injuries reported in adult and infant animals are equal
  • Ha: The number of injuries among adult animals is higher than in infants

Based on our visual interpretation of the graph we believe adult animals are more prone to injury.
